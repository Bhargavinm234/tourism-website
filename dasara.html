About Mysuru Dasara

Mysuru Dasara, the Royal Festival of Karnataka, is celebrated as a 10-day festival in Mysuru starting with nine nights called Navratri and the last day being Vijayadashami. The city celebrates this prominent and grand festival by displaying beautifully decorated elephants, camels, and horses walking together in a procession.

The festival includes various attractions such as the Jamboo Savari, exhibitions, cultural events, and the illumination of the Mysore Palace with approximately 97,000 bulbs, creating a mesmerizing sight for visitors.

Key Events

Jamboo Savari

During Jamboo Savari, elephants carry the idol of Goddess Chamundeshwari from the Mysore Palace to Bannimantap. The procession includes dances, musicals, torchlight parade, and foot soldiers accompanying the parade.

Exhibitions

The festival showcases a cultural blend through exhibitions of handicrafts, artworks, and cultural artifacts attracting visitors from across the world.
